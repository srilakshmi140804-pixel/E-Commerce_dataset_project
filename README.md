# E-Commerce_dataset_project
This project analyzes an e-commerce sales dataset of 3660 records using Microsoft Excel. Data cleaning, sales analysis, customer analysis, &amp; product performance evaluation were performed using Pivot Tables &amp; Excel functions. An interactive dashboard was created with KPI cards, charts, and slicers to visualize key business insights and sales trends.

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing e-commerce sales data using Microsoft Excel to derive meaningful business insights and support data-driven decision-making. The analysis covers sales performance, customer purchasing behavior, product performance, payment preferences, and revenue trends through a structured analytical approach.

The project involves data cleaning, transformation, exploratory analysis, and dashboard creation to convert raw transactional data into an interactive business intelligence solution.

## Business Objectives

* Evaluate overall sales performance and revenue generation.
* Identify high-performing products and categories.
* Analyze customer purchasing patterns.
* Understand payment method preferences.
* Monitor monthly and quarterly sales trends.
* Present key business metrics through an interactive dashboard.

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains transactional sales records with the following attributes:

 ## Key Business Insights

* The business generated **₹7,57,278.08** in total revenue from **3,660 orders**, with an average order value (AOV) of **₹206.91**, indicating consistent transaction sizes across purchases.

* Sales performance was well distributed across all **7 product categories**, demonstrating a balanced product portfolio. **Clothing** emerged as the highest revenue-generating category (₹1.15L), while **Electronics** recorded the lowest revenue contribution (₹1.00L).

* Customers showed a strong preference for digital payment methods. **Credit Cards** led both order volume (760 orders) and revenue generation (₹1.56L), followed closely by **UPI** and **Debit Cards**. **Cash on Delivery (COD)** was the least preferred payment option.

* Quarterly revenue remained stable at approximately **₹210K** across Q1, Q2, and Q3, indicating consistent business performance throughout most of the year.

* Monthly analysis revealed **October** as the highest-performing month with revenue of approximately **₹76K**. However, sales declined sharply by **31% in November**, highlighting a potential seasonal trend or operational issue requiring further investigation.

* Customer analysis showed a **1:1 ratio between unique customers and orders**, indicating that all purchases were made by first-time customers. This suggests a significant opportunity to improve customer retention and repeat purchase rates.

## Recommendations

* Implement a customer loyalty or rewards program to encourage repeat purchases and improve customer retention.

* Introduce product bundling and cross-selling strategies to increase the average order value beyond ₹206.91.

* Investigate the November sales decline and launch targeted promotional campaigns during low-performing periods.

* Continue promoting digital payment methods through incentives, as they represent the majority of transactions and can reduce operational costs.

* Review Q4 data collection processes and validate data completeness, particularly if December sales records appear lower than expected.
